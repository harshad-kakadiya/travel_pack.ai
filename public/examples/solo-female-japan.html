üõ°Ô∏è Solo Female Safety Tips

  • Japan is extremely safe - violent crime against tourists is virtually non-existent
  • Public transportation is safe at all hours, including for solo females
  • Women-only train cars available during rush hours (look for pink signs)
  • Bowing is important - a slight nod shows respect
  • Remove shoes when entering homes, some restaurants, and temples
  • Don't eat or drink while walking - it's considered rude
  • Keep voice low on public transport and avoid phone calls
  • Cash is king - many places don't accept credit cards

üèõÔ∏è Cultural Etiquette Guide

Essential Etiquette:

  • Bowing: Slight bow for greetings, deeper for thanks
  • Shoes: Remove when entering homes, some restaurants, temples
  • Chopsticks: Never stick upright in rice, don't pass food chopstick to chopstick
  • Temples: Bow before entering, don't touch artifacts
  • Onsen: Wash thoroughly before entering hot springs, tattoos often prohibited
  • Gift giving: Use both hands to give/receive business cards or gifts
